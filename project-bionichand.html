Flying Bionic Hand

The Flying Bionic Hand project explores the fusion of aerial robotics and biomechanical actuation. Each finger is independently driven by compact planetary geared motors, while a central palm houses a micro-drone propulsion system. The goal is to create a dexterous, semi-autonomous robotic hand capable of grasping, manipulation, and flight.

The mechanism was developed using CAD, FEA, and rapid prototyping techniques, with emphasis on torque density, mass distribution, and responsive control feedback loops. It demonstrates how flight systems can merge seamlessly with human-inspired biomechanics.
