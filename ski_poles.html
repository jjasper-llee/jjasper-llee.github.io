Custom Ski Poles

Building my own ultra-light and ultra sturdy ski pole pairs from scratch.

Background/Origin

My friends and I had planned to go ski, and I needed ski poles. No one on Facebook Marketplace had gotten back to me (and I didn't want to purcahse new ones), so I decided to make my own!

Engineering Aluminum Ski Poles

To create the end stop, I used the lathe to make two concentric cylinders with the more slender cylinder on top. I would then use the mill to drill a hole through the smaller cylinder. By adding a M4 screw through the hole, I would create a connection that could support over 400kg, a rating certainly capable of supporting me when I ski.

Engineering Carbon Fiber Ski Poles

For my carbon fiber ski poles, I decided to make them as light as possible, so instead of using alumunin, I 3D printed the end stop and handles out of TPU. Identical to the aluminum version, I used an M4 hex screw to constrain the end stop. As for the handle, I used interference fit or press fit, that uses friction and force to secure the handle in place.

Final Notes / Next Steps

After going skiing a few times with these poles, I began to feel the need to have sharper pole ends. This would evidently make it easier for me to stick the poles into the snow and take them out. A few times, when I used my poles as leverage to push myself forward, the poles would get stuck in the snow. My next ski pole pair will feature curvature similar to an industrial ski pole, allowing for more leverage and easier use.
