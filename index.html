About Me

Hi, I’m Jasper! As one with a cornucopia of interests, I thought it would be fitting for me to group my STEM-related activites in a "Left Brain" tab, and art-music-related pursuits in a "Right Brain" tab, ignoring for amusement, the well-known overlap between the two hemisphers.
An an avid cellist and maker, I believe the two "Brains" will allow me to have a well-balanced viewpoint of the world, enabling me to find the best way to blend innovation with humanity. Currently, I have been foused on human–machine interactions and aim to develop my ideas into physical products for all to have.

Left Brain

Jasper Lee is a sophomore at MIT double majoring Mechanical Engineering and Physics. His past research includes developing the first cello-fingering optimization algorithm and building an LED-powered fingerboard, along with designing, fabricating, and flying a shape-shifting tricopter drone in France through MIT’s MISTI program. As a SuperUROP researcher and HEALS Initiative Scholar in MIT’s Raman Lab, he engineers micro-topography structures for tissue regeneration to help treat neuromuscular diseases. Beyond research, Jasper is an Emerson Harris Music Scholar. He’s also active in Club Tennis, MIT Electronic Research Society, and Combat Robotics. In his free time, he enjoys playing music, racket sports, running, swimming, and spending time in makerspaces.

Right Brain

Jasper Lee made his concerto debut with the Manhattan Symphony in 2023 after winning the International Hill Concerto Competition and was recognized as a National YoungArts Winner in both 2023 and 2024. He began his musical journey on marimba at age five before transitioning to cello at nine. Since 2022, he has studied under Jonathan Koh, continuing his training with Mr. Koh as an Emerson Harris Scholar at MIT. Jasper was also admitted to the Columbia–Juilliard Dual Degree Program and to President Emeritus Laurence Lesser’s Studio at the New England Conservatory.
He has received top prizes, including first place in the CalASTA San Francisco Branch, Opus International Competition (State Round), CAPMT Concerto Competition, MTAC VOCE State Competition, International Great Composers Series, USIMC, and awards in the Houston International Competition, California American String Teachers Association, ENKOR International Competition, KAMSA Young Artist Competition, and the Junior Bach Festival.
Jasper was Co-Principal Cellist of the San Francisco Youth Symphony Orchestra and has collaborated with SF Symphony musicians at Davies Symphony Hall and Marine Corps League Foundation for annual board conferences. A two-time MTAC Panel Honor Awardee (2021 & 2022), he was invited to perform in convention masterclasses.
Through Young Chamber Musicians, Jasper was the cellist of Trio D’Neo—the first ensemble in the program to premiere a commissioned work—and of Pueri Quartet, which was selected as alternate for the National Fischoff Competition and raised over $5,000 for earthquake relief in Turkey and Syria. Both ensembles have performed at Kohl Mansion, the Rossmoor Chamber Music Society, and Channing House Senior Center.
Jasper has attended summer programs including Morningside Music Bridge, Music@Menlo Chamber Music Institute, Vivace Music Festival, CelloBello Workshop, Hidden Valley Music Seminars, and C’est Bon Chamber Music Academy. He has performed for distinguished artists such as Laurence Lesser, Paul Katz, Lluis Claret, Donald Weilerstein, Inbal Segev, Li-Wei Qin, Andres Diaz, Steven Doane, David Finckel, Darett Adkins, Natasha Brofsky, and Astrid Schween. Currently, Jasper performs with MIT’s Chamber Music Society, CelloWorld, and Ribotones.
